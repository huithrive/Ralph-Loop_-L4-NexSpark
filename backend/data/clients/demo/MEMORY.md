# YamaBushi Farms — Accumulated Learnings

## Campaign History

### Feb 15-18: Initial Meta Campaign Launch
- **What Happened:** Launched "Wagyu Enthusiasts" interest-based campaign ($50/day)
- **Result:** 2.8 ROAS, $42 CPA, 12 conversions in 3 days
- **Learning:** Interest targeting (food bloggers, grilling, Japanese cuisine) performed well
- **Action Taken:** Scaled to $75/day on Feb 19

### Feb 20: Broad Match Keyword Experiment (Google Search)
- **What Happened:** Added broad match keywords ("premium beef", "wagyu delivery") to capture more volume
- **Result:** CTR dropped from 4.2% to 1.8%, CPC spiked from $2.10 to $4.50
- **Learning:** Broad match drove irrelevant traffic (people searching for local butchers, wholesale beef)
- **Action Taken:** Paused broad match keywords Feb 21, reverted to phrase match only

### Feb 22: Creative Refresh
- **What Happened:** Introduced new ad creative featuring "Farm to Table in 48 Hours" messaging
- **Result:** Early data shows 15% higher CTR than previous "Premium Wagyu" creative
- **Learning:** Speed/convenience messaging resonates alongside quality messaging
- **Status:** Monitoring through Feb 28

## Audience Insights

### High Performers
- **Wagyu Enthusiasts (Meta):** 3.2 ROAS, $38 CPA, best overall
- **Grill Masters 40-55 (Meta):** 2.9 ROAS, $41 CPA
- **"Japanese Wagyu" Phrase Match (Google):** 3.8 ROAS, $35 CPA (small volume)

### Underperformers
- **Lookalike 1% (Meta):** 1.4 ROAS, $78 CPA — paused Feb 19
- **Broad Match Keywords (Google):** 0.9 ROAS, $95 CPA — paused Feb 21
- **Food Lovers 25-65 (Meta):** Too broad, 1.8 ROAS, $62 CPA — reduced budget 50%

## Client Preferences & Patterns

### Communication
- Prefers WhatsApp for alerts (responds within 30 min during business hours PT)
- Wants end-of-day reports via email (6 PM PT)
- Dislikes jargon — always explain metrics in plain English

### Decision Style
- Data-driven but cautious
- Prefers incremental changes over big swings
- Will approve budget increases quickly if ROAS > 3.0
- Gets nervous when daily spend exceeds $600

### Red Flags That Trigger Concern
- CPA above $50 for more than 2 days
- ROAS below 2.5
- Daily spend >$650 without his explicit approval

## Technical Notes

### Tracking Issues
- Feb 17: Noticed Meta reporting 15% fewer conversions than Shopify
- Root cause: Customers completing purchase on mobile after browsing on desktop
- Fix: Implemented 7-day click + 1-day view attribution window
- Monitoring: Still shows ~8% discrepancy (acceptable range)

## Strategic Insights

### What's Working
- Highlighting Japanese provenance and heritage resonates strongly
- Speed (48-hour delivery) is a differentiator vs competitors
- Higher-ticket items ($200+ boxes) have better ROAS than entry-level $89 box

### What's Not Working
- Generic "premium beef" messaging gets lost in noise
- Audience too broad = high spend, low conversion
- Competitor comparison ads (vs. other brands) didn't perform well

### Opportunities
- Corporate gifting angle underexplored (could be high-margin)
- Retargeting site visitors converting at 4.1 ROAS (should scale this)
- Email list (1,200 subscribers) not being used for remarketing yet

## Risk Mitigation

### Auto-Pause Rules Active
- Pause ad if ROAS < 1.5 for 24 hours
- Pause ad if CPA > $75 for 12 hours
- Alert if daily spend trajectory exceeds $650 by 4 PM PT

### Manual Review Needed
- Any budget increase >$100/day
- New audience or creative launch
- Changes to landing pages or checkout flow
